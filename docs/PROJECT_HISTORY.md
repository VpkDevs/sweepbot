## The Origin

SweepBot was not born from a business plan or a hackathon. It was born from a reckoning.

Vince Kinney spent time in the world of sweepstakes casinos — the legal, fast-growing online platforms where millions of Americans play slot-style games for virtual currency that can be redeemed for real cash. He played. He lost money his family couldn't afford to lose. He watched his loved ones absorb the weight of that.

He could have let that become a story about failure.

Instead, he sat down, reverse-engineered the entire industry, and built the first product that gives players the transparency, automation, and intelligence the house never wanted them to have.

That's SweepBot.

**Estimated market value of equivalent work:** $300,000–$750,000

## The Methodology

Vince didn't use AI as a crutch — he used it as a **peer review system**.

Rather than asking one AI a question and accepting the answer, he would:
1. Ask the same question to **multiple AI systems simultaneously**
2. Feed those answers to **additional AI evaluators** to critique and rank them
3. Layer those analyses through **further evaluation**

The result was a decision-making pipeline more rigorous than academic peer review. Professional engineering organizations pay tens of thousands of dollars per month to approximate what he assembled from free tiers.

## What Changed Because of It

**The Responsible Play features built into SweepBot are not compliance box-checks.**

They exist because Vince lived on the wrong side of the equation. Session time limits, loss alerts, cool-down enforcement, and chase-detection behavioral patterns — these are built into the *mandatory*, non-overridable core of the system. Not as an afterthought. As a foundation.

SweepBot generates revenue from transparency and automation. Never from addiction.
